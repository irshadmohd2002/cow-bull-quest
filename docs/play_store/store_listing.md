## Full description

```
Can you crack the secret word before you run out of guesses?

Cow Bull Quest is a word-deduction puzzle built on the classic Bulls and
Cows game. Instead of numbers, you're guessing letters: submit a real word,
and the game tells you how close you got.

- A Bull means a letter is correct and in the right position.
- A Cow means a letter is correct but in the wrong position.

Use those clues to narrow down the secret word, one guess at a time.

FLEXIBLE, REPLAYABLE PUZZLES
Choose 4-, 5-, or 6-letter secret words, and pick a difficulty that matches
your mood:
- Easy: familiar, high-frequency words
- Common: broader everyday vocabulary
- Hard: less frequent, trickier words

Each game gives you a limited number of attempts based on the word length
you choose, so every guess counts.

TRACK YOUR PROGRESS
Cow Bull Quest keeps local statistics on your device: wins and losses,
streaks, and a breakdown by word length and difficulty, plus a history of
your recent games. You can clear your statistics at any time from within
the app.

LIGHT, DARK, OR SYSTEM THEME
Play the way you like to look at your screen. Choose a light theme, a dark
theme, or follow your system setting automatically.

PLAY ANYWHERE, ANYTIME
Cow Bull Quest works entirely offline. There's no account to create, no
internet connection required, and no ads. Your statistics and preferences
stay on your device.

PRIVACY BY DESIGN
Cow Bull Quest does not collect personal data. There's nothing to sign up
for and nothing sent to a server — just you and the puzzle.

Ready to test your word skills? Start guessing and see how few attempts it
takes to crack the code.
```

Character count: **1,640 / 4,000**.

### Claims deliberately excluded

Per the Milestone 11 brief, the copy above does not claim: daily quests,
achievements, leaderboards, rewards, hints, AI, multiplayer, unlimited
vocabulary, educational/cognitive benefits, bug-free operation, or any
ranking/award. These are not implemented — see
`docs/play_store/branding_guide.md` ("Prohibited uses") and the Part 1
factual audit for what was checked.
